Abhyudhaya

Celebrating Unity, Diversity, and Talent
Get ready for the electrifying extravaganza of talent, thrill, and camaraderie – Abhyudhaya! Hosted by Hoysala Karnataka Sangha Hostel, get ready to immerse yourself in a whirlwind of inter-hostel events encompassing technology, culture, and sports. It's time to ignite the spirit of competition, showcase your skills, and revel in the jubilant atmosphere of Abhyudhaya!

Games

Badminton

Rules

Badminton Rules

1. Players must bring their own rackets as they will not be provided.
2. The shuttle which will be provided for the match is “Yonex Mavis 200i”.
3. The badminton court will not be of standard measurement and there will be no synthetic mat.
4. All matches follow a knockout format.
5. The game is played to 15 points in a single set.
6. If both teams reach 14 points, the first to gain 2 additional points will win the match.
7. A coin toss at the beginning of the game determines the serving side and the receiving side of the court for the teams.
8. Players are not allowed to touch the net with their racket or body at any time.
9. Serves must be cross-court (diagonally) to be valid.
10. During the serve, the shuttlecock should always be struck from below the waist or below 1.15 meters from the ground level.
11. All international rules of badminton will be applied.
12. The finals will be a 3-set game, each set played to 21 points, and the team winning 2 sets is declared the overall winner.

Code-a-Thon

Rules

Code-A-Thon

1. Registration is open for teams of two participants each.
2. In the first round, there will be a 15-minute Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) session focusing on Computer Science Fundamentals.
3. The second round involves a 1-hour coding session.
4. Qualifying participants from round 1 will receive three questions of increasing difficulty levels (easy, medium and hard).
5. Evaluation is based on the number of test cases passed and the optimality of participants' solutions.
6. The Code-A-Thon will take place on Hacker Rank.
7. Laptops and internet connections will be provided to participants.

Singing

Rules

Singing

1. The time limit is 3 minutes with an additional 1 minute.
2. The song must be only in Kannada.
3. Participants are allowed to use karaoke.
4. Karaoke submissions must be made in advance to the event organizer.
5. Rap performances are not permitted.

Treasure Hunt

Rules

Tresure Hunt

1. Participants must register in a team of 2 or 3.
2. There will be two rounds.
3. The ones selected for the second round has to come to the venue for an offline treasure hunt.
4. Prelims(online mode):
o The participants can participate remotely. So, need not come to the venue.
o Mal-practice can lead to disqualification of team.
o Further instructions will be provided prior to the start of the event.
5. Final round(offline mode):
o Starting and finishing point of the competition will be HKS hostel.
o Each team will be given clues with tasks at each location.
o Any kind of vehicle should not be used, if found team will be disqualified.
o Volunteer’s decision will be the final decision.
o Teams failing to report within the given time will be disqualified.
o Further instructions will be provided prior to the start of the event.

Hand Tennis

Rules

Hand Tennis

1. It is a one-on-one game played with a tennis ball.
2. The court resembles a circle equally divided symmetrically.
3. Each match will be for 10 points.
4. Both the hands can be used to hit the ball.
5. Each play is limited to 2 touches(pick-up and hit).
6. The ball must pitch once in circle.
7. Umpires hold complete authority over decision.
8. The specific rules of the match will be explained at the event venue.

Chess

Rules

Chess Rules

1. The game commences with a hand toss, and the player with the white pawn in hand initiates the match.
2. Each game is a knockout match.
3. Players must move the chess piece they touch.
4. Standard international chess rules will be adhered to.
5. The game has a duration of 10 minutes, with 5 minutes allotted to each player.
6. In the event of a draw, players will qualify based on their accumulated points.
7. If a player makes two illegal moves, their opponent will be declared as the winner.
8. The final match consists of three sets.
9. The referee's decision will serve as the conclusive judgment.

Quiz

Rules

Quiz Rules

1.Participants can participate in team of two.
2.There will be two rounds in the quiz.
i. Preliminary round
o There will be 20 questions with choices.
o Time limit: 20 minutes.
o Top 6 teams with highest score would be selected for final round.
ii. Final Round
o There will be 6 phases in final round, with each phase related to different fields.
o Questions should be answered in 30 seconds.
o Each question will carry 10 points.

Need for Speed

Rules

NFS Rules

* Game Version: NFS Most Wanted 2005*
1. General Competition Method: 4 players per round.
2. Rules for the tournament:
o The tournament will use a knockout format.
o The player who wins their 1st round advances to the next round.
3. Game Settings
o Courses: Circuit (Elimination rounds), Sprint (Final round).
o Car: All the players will use a common car for fair play (BMW M3 GTR).
4. Race Mode Options:
o Nitrous: On
o Collision Detection: On
5. Players are provided with a standard keyboard. Players can bring their own keyboard or controller.
6. Unfair Play:
o Use of any cheat program
o Intentional disconnection.
o Upon discovery of any player committing any violations regarded as unfair play, so that player will be disqualified from the Tournament.

BGMI

Rules

BGMI Rules

1. Each team must consist of 4 players, and all players must be physically present at the venue.
2. Players are required to bring their own mobile, as mobiles will not be provided.
3. Internet connectivity will be available for participants.
4. The use of cheats and APKs is strictly prohibited, and teams found guilty will be disqualified.
5. The competition will exclusively feature ERANGLE MAP matches.
6. No spectators are allowed during the matches.
7. The top 2 teams will be selected as the 1st and 2nd winners.

Carrom

Rules

Carrom Rules

1. The striker used in a game must be brought by the player and certified by the referee.
2. Approval from the referee is required for a change of the striker, only allowed once in a match after a particular board.
3. Powder must be evenly applied before the match begins.
4. Applying, wiping, or rubbing powder during the match is not allowed.
5. Players cannot change the position once the board is set.
6. The preliminary games consist of one set, while from quarterfinals to finals, the winners are determined based on the "Best of 3 Sets".
7. Thumb shots and striking backward are not allowed.
8. Crossing diagonal lines or pocketing the striker is a foul.
9. The striker must be in contact with the baselines to avoid a foul.
10. Players are not allowed to hold any solid materials except the striker during the match.
11.The referee's decision is final.
